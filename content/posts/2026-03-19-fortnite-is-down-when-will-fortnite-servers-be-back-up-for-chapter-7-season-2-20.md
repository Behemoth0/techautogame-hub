## When Will Fortnite Servers Be Back Up?

Historically, Epic Games takes the Fortnite servers offline at approximately 2:00 AM ET (7:00 AM UTC) for major seasonal transitions. During this time, the game client will receive a massive update—usually ranging from 15GB to 30GB depending on your platform. 

For Chapter 7 Season 2, the downtime is expected to last between 4 to 6 hours. This means that if everything goes according to plan, the servers should be back online by 8:00 AM ET / 1:00 PM UTC. However, veterans of the game know that "launch day" often comes with unforeseen hurdles. If the new mechanics are particularly complex, Epic may extend maintenance by an additional two hours to ensure stability. Keep an eye on the official @FortniteStatus Twitter (X) account for real-time updates, but 2025 trends suggest a mid-morning rollout for US players.

## How to Prepare for the Update

To ensure you are among the first to drop into the new map, follow these steps:
1. **Enable Auto-Update:** Make sure your console or PC is in sleep mode/rest mode with auto-updates enabled. This allows the 20GB+ patch to download while you sleep.
2. **Clear Storage Space:** 2025 games are huge. Ensure you have at least 50GB of free space to accommodate the update and the temporary files required for installation.
3. **Update Your Drivers:** If you are on PC, check for the latest NVIDIA or AMD drivers. Epic often collaborates with GPU manufacturers to release "Game Ready" drivers specifically for new Fortnite chapters.
