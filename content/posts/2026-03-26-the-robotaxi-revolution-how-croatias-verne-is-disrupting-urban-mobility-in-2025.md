## Introduction: The New Face of Autonomous Transit

For years, the conversation around autonomous vehicles was dominated by Silicon Valley titans like Waymo and Tesla. However, as we move through 2025, the spotlight has unexpectedly shifted to a small Mediterranean nation. Croatia, better known for its stunning coastline and the hypercar wizardry of Rimac Automobili, is now the birthplace of a startup that aims to solve the 'last mile' problem once and for all. 

Verne (formerly known as Project 3 Mobility), founded by Mate Rimac and his colleagues, isn't just building another electric car. They are building a complete ecosystem for urban mobility. With a strategic partnership with Uber now officially in the works, this little-known startup is positioned to leapfrog established players by integrating directly into the world's most popular ride-hailing app.

## The Verne Philosophy: Comfort Over Capacity

Unlike the utilitarian pods we’ve seen from Amazon’s Zoox or the modified sedans from Waymo, the Verne robotaxi is a radical departure from traditional automotive design. It is a two-seater. This choice was intentional; data shows that the vast majority of ride-hailing trips involve only one or two passengers. By eliminating the back seat and the steering wheel, Verne has created a cabin that feels more like a high-end living room or a first-class airplane suite than a car.

Inside, passengers are treated to massive legroom, reclining seats, and a 43-inch widescreen display that handles everything from entertainment to trip information. The absence of pedals and a dashboard allows for a completely flat floor, maximizing the sense of space. It’s a bold bet: that commuters value personal space and luxury over the ability to carry four people they don't have with them anyway.

## The Uber Partnership: A Scalability Masterstroke

While building a cool autonomous car is one thing, building a global ride-hailing network is another entirely. This is where the partnership with Uber becomes critical. Rather than trying to build a brand-new user base from scratch—a move that has cost competitors billions—Verne will allow users to summon their robotaxis directly through the Uber app in launch cities.

For Uber, this provides a steady stream of autonomous 'supply' without the overhead of vehicle maintenance. For Verne, it provides instant access to millions of users. This synergy is expected to accelerate the rollout of Verne’s services across Europe and the Middle East, starting with their pilot program in Zagreb before expanding to the UK and Germany by late 2025 and early 2026.

## The Tech Under the Hood: Mobileye and the 'Mothership'

Verne isn't trying to reinvent the wheel when it comes to the 'eyes' of the vehicle. They have partnered with Mobileye, utilizing the Mobileye Drive platform. This system uses a sophisticated suite of LiDAR, radar, and cameras to achieve Level 4 autonomy. By using a proven platform, Verne can focus on the user experience and the infrastructure.

Speaking of infrastructure, Verne is introducing the 'Mothership.' These are specialized hubs located in every city where the fleet operates. At the Mothership, the vehicles are autonomously cleaned, inspected, and charged. This ensures that every time a Verne pulls up to your curb, it feels brand new—addressing one of the biggest complaints about current ride-sharing services: vehicle cleanliness.

## The Challenges Ahead: Regulation and Trust

Despite the excitement, Verne faces significant hurdles. Regulatory approval for a vehicle with no manual overrides is notoriously difficult in the European Union. Furthermore, there is the 'trust factor.' Public perception of autonomous vehicles took a hit in 2024 following several high-profile incidents involving other brands. Verne will need to prove that its Mobileye-powered system is not just as good as a human driver, but significantly better.

There is also the question of infrastructure. The 'Mothership' concept requires significant real estate in densely populated urban centers, which isn't cheap. However, with the backing of the Rimac Group and the logistical might of Uber, Verne has a better shot than most at making this work.

## Bottom Line / Our Verdict

Verne is the most exciting development in the autonomous space we’ve seen in years. By focusing on a high-end, two-passenger experience and leveraging Uber’s massive network, they have avoided the pitfalls of their predecessors.

While we wait for the first fleet to hit the streets, now is the time to upgrade your mobile tech kit to match the future of transport.
