## Introduction: The Golden Age of Looting is Back

With the highly anticipated release of Borderlands 4 in 2025, Vault Hunters across the galaxy are already preparing for the inevitable: a backpack full of legendary weapons and nowhere to put them. If there is one thing Gearbox Software has mastered, it is the art of the 'loot drop,' and in this latest installment, the sheer volume of gear is staggering. 

Whether you are hunting for the perfect elemental roll on a Torgue shotgun or hoarding rare class mods for a secondary character, knowing exactly where the bank locations are is vital for your progression. In this guide, we will break down every confirmed bank location in Borderlands 4, how to maximize your storage space, and the best hardware to ensure your inventory management is as smooth as your gunplay.

## The Central Hub: The Bastion Bank

Unlike previous entries that saw us floating through space on Sanctuary III, Borderlands 4 introduces 'The Bastion'—a sprawling planetary fortress that serves as your primary base of operations. The main bank is located in the **Aegis District**, specifically inside the 'Hoard-O-Matic' facility.

This is your primary storage unit. It is a shared bank, meaning any item you place here can be accessed by any of your other Vault Hunters. This is the most important feature for players looking to 'power-level' secondary characters. If you find a high-tier sniper rifle that doesn't fit your current Siren build but would be perfect for a Sniper-class character, the Bastion Bank is where it belongs.

## Regional Outposts and Quick-Access Vaults

Gearbox has listened to player feedback regarding the long treks back to the main hub. In Borderlands 4, they have introduced **Regional Vaults**. These are smaller banking kiosks found in major planetary settlements. While they don't offer the full visual display of your collection, they allow you to deposit items directly into your global bank without leaving the combat zone.

Key Regional Vault locations include:
1. **The Dust-Bowl Outpost (Pandora Revisited):** Located right next to the Catch-A-Ride station in the central canyon.
2. **Neon City Sub-Level (Promethea II):** Found inside the 'Safe Zone' bar near the fast travel point.
3. **The Overgrowth Research Station:** Tucked behind the vending machines in the main laboratory.

## How to Upgrade Your Bank Space (SDUs)

In 2025, inventory management remains a core loop of the Borderlands experience. You will start with a modest 20 slots in your bank, which will fill up within the first few hours of gameplay. To expand this, you must visit **Marcus Munitions** on The Bastion.

Marcus sells Storage Deck Upgrades (SDUs) for cold, hard cash. Be warned: while the first few upgrades are cheap, the prices scale exponentially. By the time you reach the final 'Max Capacity' tier (which offers 500 slots), you will need millions of credits. We recommend prioritizing Bank SDUs over Grenade or Pistol SDUs in the early game to ensure you never have to leave a Legendary item behind on the battlefield.

## Pro-Tips for Inventory Management

With the new 'Quick-Sort' feature in Borderlands 4, managing your bank is easier than ever. You can now tag items as 'Junk' or 'Favorite' directly from the bank interface. 

*   **The Mules:** If you run out of bank space even after maxing out your SDUs, the community-favorite 'Mule' strategy returns. Create a new character, run them to The Bastion, and use their personal backpack as extra storage.
*   **Wall Mounts:** Don't forget that your personal quarters in The Bastion feature weapon racks. These don't count toward your bank limit, allowing you to show off your rarest finds while freeing up space.
