## The Pixar Revolution Comes to the Inklands

Since its explosive debut, Disney Lorcana has been the talk of the trading card game (TCG) world. While Ravensburger has spent the last year establishing the foundational lore of the Great Illuminary, fans have been clamoring for one thing: Pixar. The wait is officially over. In a massive reveal that has sent shockwaves through the competitive and casual communities alike, 32 new cards have been unveiled, featuring the long-awaited arrival of Toy Story, The Incredibles, and Brave. 

As we head into 2025, these additions aren't just cosmetic; they represent a fundamental shift in how the game is played. The introduction of these franchises brings new mechanics, thematic synergies, and a level of power creep that will force even the most seasoned Ruby/Amethyst players to rethink their strategies. From the high-flying heroics of Buzz Lightyear to the tactical archery of Merida, the scope of the Disney Lorcana universe just expanded exponentially.

## Toy Story: To Infinity and Beyond the Inkwell

The Toy Story reveal is undoubtedly the centerpiece of this 32-card drop. Fans have been theorizing how Woody and Buzz would fit into the Inklands since the game was first announced. The new cards lean heavily into the concept of "Togetherness" and "Support."

**Buzz Lightyear - Space Ranger (Steel):** This card is a powerhouse. With a high willpower and the 'Challenger' keyword, Buzz is designed to take down high-threat targets without breaking a sweat. His ability, 'Falling with Style,' allows him to move damage counters to opposing characters whenever he is challenged, making him a defensive nightmare.

**Woody - Favorite Deputy (Amber):** Woody acts as the ultimate engine for Amber decks. His 'Gather the Crew' ability allows players to search the top five cards of their deck for a 'Toy' character and add it to their hand. This level of consistency is exactly what Amber decks have been missing, potentially making 'Toy' tribal decks a top-tier archetype in the 2025 competitive circuit.

## The Incredibles: A Masterclass in Synergy

If Toy Story is about support, The Incredibles cards are all about family synergy. The Parr family introduces a new mechanic where cards gain bonuses based on the presence of other family members on the board. 

**Mr. Incredible - Strength in Numbers (Ruby):** Bob Parr is a heavy hitter. While his base stats are impressive, he gains +2 Strength for every other 'Incredible' character you have in play. This makes a full board of supers almost impossible to contest in a straight-up challenge.

**Elastigirl - Flexible Fighter (Emerald):** Helen brings the 'Evasive' keyword to the forefront. Her ability to reach across the board and challenge Evasive characters—while remaining difficult to hit herself—provides Emerald decks with a much-needed counter to the current 'Evasive' meta dominance.

**Jack-Jack - Wild Card (Amethyst):** True to his character, Jack-Jack is unpredictable. He has a randomized ability that triggers at the start of your turn, potentially granting him 'Rush,' 'Ward,' or even 'Draw a Card.' It’s a high-risk, high-reward playstyle that Amethyst players will love.

## Brave: Merida and the Fate of the Emerald Ink

Merida’s inclusion brings a breath of fresh air to the Emerald and Steel inks. As a character, Merida is defined by her independence, and her card reflects that. 

**Merida - Skilled Archer (Emerald):** Merida introduces a 'Precision' mechanic. When she challenges a character, the player can choose to deal her damage to a different character in the same zone. This allows for tactical snipes against weakened backline characters that are usually protected by 'Bodyguard' units. For players who enjoy a control-oriented playstyle, Merida is going to be an auto-include.

## How the 2025 Meta is Shifting

With 32 new cards entering the fray, the meta is currently in a state of flux. The most significant change is the introduction of 'Tribal' synergies. Previously, decks were built around colors and broad mechanics (like 'Discard' or 'Healing'). Now, with enough Toy Story and Incredibles cards, we are seeing the rise of specific character-group decks. 

Furthermore, the power level of these new Pixar cards seems slightly higher than the First Chapter staples. This is a common trend in TCGs to keep the game fresh, but it means collectors and players need to act fast to secure these cards before the 2025 tournament season kicks off in earnest.
