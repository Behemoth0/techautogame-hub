## The 2025 MOT Surge: Don't Get Caught Out

For UK motorists, the 'MOT season' usually peaks in March and September, coinciding with the release of new registration plates. However, 2025 has seen a significant backlog due to the lingering effects of previous years' extensions and the increasing complexity of modern vehicle sensors. An MOT (Ministry of Transport) test is mandatory for vehicles over three years old, and with the average cost of a fail rising due to inflation and parts scarcity, preparation is key.

This year, the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) has placed a renewed focus on emissions and ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems). If your lane-keep assist or autonomous braking sensors are showing a fault code, it's an automatic fail. 

To ensure you pass the first time, we recommend a DIY pre-check. Check your wipers, top up your screen wash, and most importantly, inspect your tyres. A significant percentage of MOT failures in 2025 are still down to basic maintenance issues that could have been fixed for under £20. If you are driving an older EV, pay special attention to suspension components; the extra weight of battery packs means bushings and ball joints are wearing out faster than on traditional ICE cars.

## The New BMW i3: A 'Neue Klasse' Revolution

The original BMW i3 was a quirky, carbon-fibre-reinforced hatchback that was ahead of its time. For 2025, however, the i3 nameplate has been reborn. This isn't just a facelift; it is the flagship of BMW’s 'Neue Klasse' (New Class) platform. The 2025 BMW i3 is now an all-electric sedan, essentially the EV twin to the iconic 3 Series, and it aims to set the benchmark for driving dynamics in the electric era.

What makes the new i3 special? It moves away from the prismatic battery cells used in previous i-models to new cylindrical cells. This change alone offers a 20% increase in energy density and a 30% boost in range. We are looking at a real-world range of approximately 370 to 400 miles on a single charge. 

Inside, the cabin is a minimalist's dream. BMW has introduced 'Panoramic Vision,' a head-up display that spans the entire width of the windscreen, controlled by gesture and voice. It feels less like a car and more like a high-end lounge. The new i3 is BMW's direct answer to the Tesla Model 3 and the Hyundai Ioniq 6, and from what we have seen on the test tracks, the Munich-based manufacturer has reclaimed its 'Ultimate Driving Machine' title for the digital age.

## Goodwood Festival of Speed 2025: The Cathedral of Velocity

No automotive year is complete without a pilgrimage to the Duke of Richmond’s estate for the Goodwood Festival of Speed. As we look toward the 2025 event, the theme is 'The Evolution of Power.' Goodwood remains one of the few places on earth where you can see a 1920s Bugatti hill-climbing right before a 2,000hp electric hypercar.

For 2025, the 'Electric Avenue' has been expanded to include more affordable EVs, reflecting the shift in the market. However, the stars of the show will undoubtedly be the hydrogen-combustion prototypes and the latest Formula 1 machinery. Rumours suggest that several teams will be bringing their 2025 chassis for a public run-up the hill, giving fans an early look at the aero-packages that will dominate the season.

If you are attending, the 'Future Lab' is a must-visit. This year, it focuses on VTOL (Vertical Take-Off and Landing) aircraft and AI-driven logistics. It is a reminder that while we love the cars on the track, the technology under the hood is moving toward a much broader horizon. Goodwood isn't just a race; it’s a vision of what mobility looks like in the next decade.
