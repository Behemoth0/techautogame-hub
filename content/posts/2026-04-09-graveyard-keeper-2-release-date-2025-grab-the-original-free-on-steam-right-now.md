## The Dead Rise Again: Graveyard Keeper 2 is Coming in 2025

If you have ever felt that Stardew Valley was just a bit too cheerful and lacked a sufficient amount of corpse disposal, then you likely found a home in the dark, hilarious, and deeply addictive world of Graveyard Keeper. Developed by Lazy Bear Games, the original title became a cult sensation for its unique blend of medieval cemetery management, unethical business practices, and surprisingly deep crafting trees. Now, the news fans have been waiting for is finally here: Graveyard Keeper 2 is officially slated for a 2025 release.

But the news gets even better for those who haven't yet dipped their toes into the business of organ harvesting and headstone carving. ## Why You Need to Claim the Original Graveyard Keeper Now

Graveyard Keeper is often described as the most inaccurate medieval cemetery management sim of all time, and that is precisely why it works. You play as a man transported to a mysterious medieval world where you are tasked with maintaining a dilapidated cemetery. However, the game quickly expands into a sprawling RPG where you must navigate the politics of the local Church, brew questionable potions, and deal with a talking skull named Gerry who has a penchant for wine.

The complexity of the game is its greatest strength. You aren't just digging holes; you are managing a supply chain that involves blacksmithing, alchemy, writing sermons, and even running a local tavern. The dark humor permeates every interaction, making the 'grind' feel rewarding rather than tedious. By claiming the original game now for free, you are giving yourself the perfect primer for the expanded mechanics promised in the 2025 sequel.

## What We Know About Graveyard Keeper 2

While details are still emerging, Lazy Bear Games has hinted that Graveyard Keeper 2 will take everything that made the first game a success and scale it up significantly. We are looking at a 2025 launch that promises a more refined engine, better automation for late-game tasks, and a significantly larger map to explore. 

One of the biggest criticisms of the first game was the 'walking simulator' aspect during the early hours. Rumors suggest that the sequel will introduce more robust fast-travel systems and perhaps even mounts. Furthermore, the crafting system—already legendary for its depth—is expected to include more branching paths, allowing players to focus more on specific 'industries' like winemaking, corpse-augmentation, or dark magic.
